## ğŸ¯ What This Is

A modular, team-centered approach to scaling support operations from 7 analysts to 15+ while handling doubled business volume. Built on actual observations from three rounds of interviews with FieldStack's support team, account management, and leadership.

**Core principles:**
- Trust before change - Build relationships before implementing new processes
- Team as authors - Make existing analysts the architects of their own success
- Empower, don't control - Systems that enable independence, not approval theater
- Strategic asset - Position support as competitive advantage, not cost center

## ğŸ“¦ What's Included

### Executive Summary
One-page overview with visual module dependency map showing how trust building, quick wins, documentation, metrics, scaling prep, and strategic positioning connect.

### Full 90-Day Plan
Six modular components that can be implemented flexibly based on actual team needs:

1. **Trust Building** - Individual 1-on-1s, workflow documentation, shadowing, training review
2. **Quick Wins** - Fix broken processes, establish escalation paths, rotating responsibilities
3. **Documentation** - Process SOPs, knowledge bases (internal and customer-facing), ticket categorization
4. **Metrics & Data** - Ticket lifecycle clarity, duplicate ticket handling, dashboard, proactive client communication
5. **Scaling Preparation** - Training infrastructure (3 phases), enterprise client playbook, hiring strategy
6. **Strategic Positioning** - Account Management partnership, product feedback loop, escalation framework

### Appendix A: Data Strategy
Post-90-day strategic initiative exploring support team's access to customer data, liability protection, governance frameworks, and scalability from 30 to 30,000+ customers.

## ğŸ’¡ Background

This plan was developed based on three interview rounds where I learned about:
- Two previous failures in this role (internal hire lacking leadership skills, external hire who couldn't learn the business)
- Upcoming growth: Two large retail clients expected to double year-over-year business
- Current challenges: Duplicate tickets from large clients, customer dependency on specific analysts, unclear escalation paths, no formal knowledge base
- Team dynamics: 7 technical support analysts with deep product knowledge but limited process structure

The plan addresses these specific challenges while building sustainable systems for growth.
